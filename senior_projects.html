Senior Projects

There are two types of projects offered mainly to seniors: Capstone projects and Course projects.

Capstone projects

Capstone projects come from Capstone courses that are usually the last upper-division courses required for seniors to graduate. They last for one semester, are typically sponsored by a third-party company or individual, and focus on teams developing real-life solutions for the client's needs.

Sponsors

Sponsors play a vital role in shaping the future of technology while addressing real-world challenges faced by businesses and organizations. They are granted the opportunity to work with students at CSUSM to not only tackle challenges, but also help them build their job experience by the time they graduate.

Expectations as a sponsor

1. Project Collaboration

Sponsors have the opportunity to engage with a team of talented CS/CIS students who will work on a project aimed at delivering tangible solutions for your business needs. These projects can vary from developing software applications, designing websites, and implementing systems, to addressing specific technological challenges. The size of the teams are based on the scope of the project and include 4-6 students in each team. Sponsors are encouraged to maintain regular communication with their assigned teams throughout the project duration (Fall or Spring semester). This includes providing guidance, and feedback, and ensuring alignment with project goals. Additionally, sponsors can schedule meetings as needed to discuss progress, milestones, and any adjustments required.

2. Project Timeline

If you submit interest for the Fall semester, teams will start their work from September to December. For the Spring semester, projects run from February to May.

3. Benefits of Sponsorship

  • Access to current perspectives and innovative ideas from aspiring CS/CIS professionals.
  • Solutions tailored to address specific business challenges or technological needs.
  • Opportunity to network with students, faculty, and industry professionals.
  • Recognition for contributing to the academic and professional development of future tech leaders.

4. Project Showcase

At the culmination of each semester, there is a formal gallery-style presentation where sponsors, faculty, students, and community members are invited to view projects. This serves as an opportunity to showcase the hard work, innovation, and impact achieved through collaboration.

Become a sponsor!

If you want to sponsor a project, click the Sign Up button on the left pane (or bottom pane for mobile users) and we will assist you in getting the sponsorship up and running.

For CIS-related projects, the sponsor form must be submitted by the end of January. Students are expected to form teams for each sponsor project by the first two weeks in the semester. The projects will be expected to be done by the end of May.

For CS-related projects, the sponsor form must be submitted by the end of January for the Spring semester or by the end of August for the Fall semester. The projects are expected to be completed by May for the Spring semester or December for the Fall semester.

Course projects

Course projects come from courses that are not part of our Capstone program. These courses can include electives such as Game Programming (CS 485) and Software Engineering (CS 370). While course projects are typically not sponsored by a third-party and are typically assigned by the course instructors, they still test the students' ability to utilize what they learned in the course to solve real-world problems.
