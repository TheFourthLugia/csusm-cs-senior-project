Senior Projects

There are two types of projects offered mainly to seniors: Capstone projects and Course projects.

Capstone projects

Capstone projects come from Capstone courses that are usually the last upper-division courses required for seniors to graduate. They are typically sponsored by a third-party company or individual and focus on teams developing real-life solutions for the client's needs.

More info

Become a sponsor!

Our Capstone program provides senior students with the challenge and training to solve real-life business problems such as developing/maintaining information systems, data processing, and web development. It also provides sponsors the opportunity to solve their own internal challenges through student help and the satisfaction that they are training students for a better tomorrow.

If you want to sponsor a project, click the Sign Up button on the left pane (or bottom pane for mobile users).

For CIS-related projects, the sponsor form must be submitted by the end of January. Students are expected to form teams for each sponsor project by the first two weeks in the semester. The projects will be expected to be done by the end of May.

For CS-related projects, the sponsor form must be submitted by the end of January for the Spring semester or by the end of August for the Fall semester. The projects are expected to be completed by May for the Spring semester or December for the Fall semester.

Course projects

Course projects come from courses that are not part of our Capstone program. These courses can include electives such as Game Programming (CS 485) and Software Engineering (CS 370). While course projects are typically not sponsored by a third-party and are typically assigned by the course instructors, they still test the students' ability to utilize what they learned in the course to solve real-world problems.
