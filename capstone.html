Capstone Project

A capstone project is a semester-long or year-long course that involves students working in groups to develop and deliver a project for a CSUSM sponsor from a third-party company or individual. The project can cover a wide range of topics, from software development to machine learning to cybersecurity. Throughout the project, students apply the skills and knowledge they have gained in their computer science coursework to real-world problems and challenges.

This often involves working closely with the sponsor to understand their needs and requirements, developing a plan and timeline for the project, and using various programming tools and technologies to build and test the solution. At the end of the semester, the students present their project to the sponsor and showcase their work to faculty and peers.

This experience provides students with valuable hands-on learning and professional development opportunities, as well as the chance to make meaningful contributions to real-world projects.

Types of Capstone Projects

There are three types of Capstone Projects that students will usually deal with:

  • Industry - Projects that are sponsored by a third-party business firm
  • Educational Institutions - Projects that are sponsored by a school, college, or any other education firms
  • Facilty/Student - Projects that are sponsored by a facility or a student at CSUSM

Available Capstone Courses

Computer Information Systems

  • CIS 490 - Project Management and Practice
    Units: 3
    Pre-requisite: CIS 444

    Advanced CIS majors operating as a high-performance team will engage in and complete the design and implementation of a significant information system. Project management, management of the CIS function, and systems integration will be components of the project experience.

    This course can only be taken in the Spring semester.

Computer Science

  • CS 490 - Capstone Project
    Units: 3
    Pre-requisite: At least two CS 400-level courses completed

    Assigns team-based projects with realistic constraints from clients. Engages students in incremental software development process, periodically conducting project and code reviews, and frequently collecting feedback from stakeholders. Provides hands-on experience in managing projects, making team decisions, documenting design artifacts, applying modern construction technologies, as well as presenting and demonstrating their project progress to stakeholders.

    Each lecture section is limited to 40 students; each lab section is limited to 15 students.
    Two hours of lecture. Three hours of laboratory.

FAQs

  • What events are there?
    A capstone project typically involves a final presentation and a showcase. At the end of the spring semester, the students give formal final presentations to demonstrate their project design/progress/results. The business sponsor is invited to attend.
  • How long does a capstone project last?
    A capstone project lasts from one semester to one year depending on the major.
  • Do I need to have a specific topic in mind for my capstone project?
    Most projects will have their sponsors listed, while groups can decide which one to complete. Some projects may use ideas submitted by students.
  • Can I work on a capstone project alone, or do I need to work in a group?
    Most capstone projects are designed to be completed by teams to emphasize the importance of teamwork in a career environment.
  • What kind of support will I receive during my capstone project?
    Students will receive guidance and support from a faculty advisor or project sponsor as well as access to any necessary resources or tools.
  • How is the capstone project graded?
    Capstone projects are graded based on factors such as the quality and completeness of the final product, the level of creativity and innovation demonstrated, and the ability to effectively communicate the project's goals and outcomes. Ensuring timely success is also part of the project grade, meaning timely meetings and scheduled reports are necessary for a good grade.
  • Can I choose my own project sponsor, or will one be assigned to me?
    CSUSM sponsors are appointed for students.
